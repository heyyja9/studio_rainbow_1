Origami Bracelet

Materials Needed:

● Rainbow loom

● Rainbow loom hook

● Black rubber bands

● Magenta rubber bands

● Dark green rubber bands

● C-clip

Step One

Position your loom so that the red arrow points away from you. The middle column should be one space lower than the left and right columns.

Step Two

Take a black rubber band and stretch it from the middle peg at the bottom of the loom to the top right peg. This will be the colour of the first chain.

Step Three

Starting from the last peg where you left off, connect another black rubber band vertically to the peg in front. Repeat this step with additional black bands until you’ve connected them all the way to the top of the loom.

Step Four

Starting from the bottom left peg, take a magenta rubber band and stretch it horizontally to the far right peg. Repeat this step across the rest of the loom until you reach the second-to-last row (just before the final black rubber band). Magenta will be the colour of the loops that enclose the second chain.

Step Five

Return to the bottom of the loom again, take a dark green rubber band, and connect it vertically from the bottom middle peg to the peg in front. Continue connecting dark green bands vertically up the middle column until you reach the top. This will be the colour of the chain enclosed inside the loops.

Step Six

Once you reach the end of the loom, connect the last dark green band to the top right peg.

Step Seven

At this same top right peg, wrap a black rubber band around it twice to create a cap band. This will keep your bracelet secure, and provides a place for the C-clip to attach to later on.

Step Eight

Rotate your loom so the red arrow points towards you.

Step Nine

Starting from the bottom left peg, use your hook to grab the dark green band and loop it over to the top middle peg.

Step Ten

Then, at each peg from here, grab the bottom dark green band and hook it forward to the peg in front. Continue this process until you’ve reached the end of the loom.

Step Eleven

Return to the bottom of the loom. On the right peg, use your hook to grab the horizontal magenta band and pull it over to the far left peg. Repeat this for the rest of the magenta bands across the loom.

Step Twelve

At the bottom left peg of the loom, use your hook to grab the black band under the cap band, and connect it to the peg in front. Then, at each peg from here, grab the bottom black band, and hook it forwards to the peg in front. Make sure you pull back the magenta rubber bands first before grabbing these black bands. Continue this until you reach the end of the loom.

Step Thirteen

When you reach the last peg of the left column, stretch the black band diagonally to the top middle peg.

Step Fourteen

At this same top middle peg, insert your hook through all the rubber bands on the peg. Grab a dark green rubber band and pull it through all the bands, then place both ends of it onto the middle of your hook to create a secure link for later.

Step Fifteen

Carefully pull your design off the loom.

Step Sixteen

To create the extension, position the loom so the red arrow points towards you. Attach as many dark green bands as needed vertically, from the top peg down to the bottom peg, so the bracelet fits around your wrist.

Step Seventeen

Once all your dark green rubber bands are placed, loop the link from step 12 onto the last peg at the bottom of the loom. Then, use your hook to grab the ottom dark green band on each peg and loop it forward to the peg in front, until you reach the top of the loom.

Step Eighteen

Attach a C-clip to the bands on the top peg. Once secured, carefully remove the bands from the peg and pull the extension off the loom.

Step Nineteen

Connect the same C-clip to the cap band on the opposite side of the bracelet (the initial black cap band) to close it.

Step Twenty

Finished! Flaunt your super cool origami bracelet!
